## From a Missed Opportunity to a Meaningful Beginning

My journey with Sudarshan Sir didn’t begin with the Vinternship.  
It started much earlier, through “The Joy of Computing Using Python”, one of the four courses that helped me earn the NPTEL Superstar recognition. That course left a lasting impact on me — the clarity, simplicity, and joy he brought into programming made learning feel effortless.

So when I attended the NPTEL Star Workshop on February 1st at IIT Madras and heard Sudarshan Sir speak about the Vinternship, it immediately caught my attention. I wanted to join right away, but with GATE preparation underway, I knew I couldn’t commit. I left the event with a small regret, feeling like I had missed something meaningful.

But life has a way of bringing opportunities back.

Soon after the GATE exam, I received an email from NPTEL announcing the Vinternship as part of a course by Sudarshan Sir. This time, I didn’t hesitate. I joined the AKSians cohort, ready to give it my full focus.

---

## ViBe — A Different Way of Learning

During the welcome ceremony, the meaning behind the Vi-Be platform (Vikram–Betal) was explained. That analogy stayed with me. It wasn’t just a platform; it was a mindset — a way of learning through questioning, reflection, and dialogue.

Motivated by that idea, I completed all the ViBe videos even before the second week began. It felt like a strong start… until the real test arrived.

---

## Case Studies — Where Concepts Turn Into Skills

The case studies were where everything changed for me.

They weren’t typical assignments with fixed answers. They demanded:

- Deep understanding  
- Patience  
- Trial and error  
- And the willingness to think beyond the obvious  

Every case study pushed me out of my comfort zone. It made me realize the difference between *knowing* a concept and *applying* it.

The breakout sessions became incredibly valuable. Listening to different approaches, discussing doubts, and seeing how others reasoned through problems helped me grow faster than I expected. I genuinely look forward to more of these discussions.

---

## Hands‑On Learning With Mentors

Another highlight of the Vinternship was the hands on live sessions with instructors like Riya Mehta.  
Her step by step explanations and practical demonstrations made complex topics feel approachable. These sessions bridged the gap between theory and real‑world application, especially when I felt stuck.

---

## CoderHigh — A Bonus Push

Along the way, I also attended a few sessions of CoderHigh.  
Even though I couldn’t attend regularly, those sessions added an extra spark — a different teaching style, a different pace, and a different way of thinking about problems. It complemented the Vinternship experience beautifully.

---


## A New Technical World Opened Up

Through the Vinternship, I was introduced to modern technologies that I had only heard about before but never had the chance to explore deeply:

- JavaScript  
- TypeScript  
- React.js  
- MongoDB  
- Express.js

One thing that helped me tremendously was the MERN videos in ViBe by Nandan Prabhudesai . His explanations were crisp, structured, and beginner‑friendly. Whenever I felt stuck or overwhelmed, revisiting his sessions gave me clarity. It felt like having a mentor who understood exactly where learners struggle and guided us step by step through the modern web development stack.

Working with these tools made me realize how much the industry has evolved — and how important it is to keep learning and reskilling. Each technology brought its own challenges, but also its own excitement.

---

## Balancing Work and Learning

Being a working professional, weekends are my main learning time. The live sessions on Saturdays and Sundays were extremely helpful, but I couldn’t attend all Saturday sessions due to work commitments. I usually get only the second and fourth Saturdays off, so I rely heavily on recorded videos.

Sometimes the recordings get delayed, and when Sunday’s session continues from Saturday’s discussion, it becomes challenging to catch up. Still, I try to stay consistent and make the most of whatever time I have.

---

## My Progress So Far

I’ve completed the first 18 TypeScript case studies.  
Case Study 19 was particularly tough. Instead of getting stuck, I decided to explore other tracks like MongoDB and React, planning to return to it with a fresh perspective.

This flexibility — the freedom to pause, explore, and return — is something Vinternship has taught me.  
**Learning isn’t a straight line. It’s a cycle of revisiting, rethinking, and rebuilding.**

---

## Looking Ahead

From discovering Sudarshan Sir’s courses to attending the NPTEL Star Workshop, and finally becoming part of the Vinternship, this journey has been transformative. It has challenged me, reshaped my thinking, and helped me grow in ways I didn’t expect.

I’m excited for the upcoming weeks — more case studies, more discussions, more breakthroughs, and more moments of clarity.
